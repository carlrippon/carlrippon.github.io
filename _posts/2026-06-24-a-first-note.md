This is where I plan to write short pieces about my research: the economics of disasters in small, open island economies, and what the results mean once you step away from the equations.

Most of my work lives in models and regressions. The idea here is to do the opposite for a moment: take one result at a time and explain it plainly. Why output can keep falling long after a storm has passed. Why the capital that survives a hurricane often matters more than the capital it destroys. How a single chart, the impulse response, sits behind most of what I do.

No fixed schedule, no fixed length. Just notes.
